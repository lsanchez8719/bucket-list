Cairo

Cairo, the capital of Egypt, is a city brimming with ancient history and iconic landmarks. One of the oldest sites in Cairo is the Great Pyramid of Giza, built as a tomb for the pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BCE. It is the last remaining wonder of the ancient world. Another notable site is the Egyptian Museum, which houses an extensive collection of artifacts, including treasures from Tutankhamun's tomb.

Amsterdam

Amsterdam, the capital of the Netherlands, is renowned for its picturesque canals, artistic heritage, and vibrant culture. One of the oldest sites in Amsterdam is the Oude Kerk (Old Church), founded in the 13th century. It is the city's oldest building and a remarkable example of medieval architecture. Another notable site is the Anne Frank House, where Anne Frank and her family hid during World War II, providing a poignant glimpse into the history of that era.

Stiklestad

Stiklestad, located in Norway, holds significant historical and cultural importance due to its connection to the Viking Age. The oldest site in Stiklestad is Stiklestad Church, dating back to the 12th century and built on the spot where the Battle of Stiklestad took place in 1030. This battle marked the final conflict of King Olaf II, and the church stands as a memorial to his legacy. Stiklestad is also home to the Stiklestad National Cultural Center, a museum that explores the Viking heritage and the Battle of Stiklestad.
